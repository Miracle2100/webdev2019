Easy Pork Chop Bake with Creamy Sauce

  • Time:70 mins
  • Serves:4
Ingredients Quantity
pork chops(1/2 to 3/4 inch thick) 4
tablespoons vegetable oil 2
Kosher salt and pepper 1
can cream of chicken soup 1
medium onion, sliced 1
tablespoons ketchup 3
teaspoons Worcestershire sauce 2

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 350 F.

Step 2: Heat the oil in a skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat; add the pork chops and sear, turning to brown both sides.

Step 3: Season the chops with salt and pepper and drain off any excess fat.

Step 4: Transfer the seared pork chops to a baking dish if the skillet is not oven safe.

Step 5: In a bowl, combine the cream of chicken soup, sliced onion, ketchup, and Worcestershire sauce; Stir to blend thoroughly and then pour the sauce over the pork chops.

Step 6: Cover the skillet or baking dish tightly with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 45 to 60 minutes, or until chops are tender.

Step 7: Serve these pork chops with potatoes and steamed broccoli, spinach, or green beans.

Pressure Cooker Chocolate POTS DE CRÈME

  • Time:4 hours
  • Serves:6
Ingredients Quantity
cups heavy cream 11/2
cup whole milk 1/2
large egg yolks 5
cup sugar 1/4
pinch of salt 1
ounces bittersweet chocolate, melted 8
whipped cream and grated chocolate for decoration, optional 1

Step 1: In a small saucepan, bring the cream and milk to a simmer.

Step 2: In a large mixing bowl, whisk together egg yolks, sugar, and salt. Slowly whisk in the hot cream and milk. Whisk in chocolate until blended. Pour into 6 custard cups. (I used 1/2 pint mason jars.)

Step 3: Add 1 1/2 cups of water to the pressure cooker and place the trivet in the bottom. Place 3 cups on the trivet and place a second trivet on top of the cups. Stack the remaining three cups on top of the second trivet.

Step 4: Lock the lid in place. Select High Pressure and set the timer for 6 minutes. When beep sounds, turn off pressure cooker and use a natural pressure release for 15 minutes and then do a quick pressure release to release any remaining pressure. When valve drops carefully remove lid.

Step 5: Carefully remove the cups to a wire rack to cool uncovered. When cool, refrigerate covered with plastic wrap for at least 4 hours or overnight.

Homemade Worcestershire Sauce

  • Time:5 hrs 35 mins
  • Serves:96 (6 cups)
Ingredients Quantity
tablespoons olive oil 2
large sweet onions, roughly chopped 2
cup tamarind paste 1/2
tablespoons minced garlic 2
tablespoons minced ginger 2
jalapeños, seeds removed and minced 2
cup anchovies, chopped 1/4
cup tomato paste 1/4
whole cloves 2
tablespoons freshly cracked black pepper 2
cup dark corn syrup 1/2
cup molasses 1
cups white vinegar 3
cup dark beer 1
cup orange juice 1/2
cups water 2
lemon, thinly sliced 1
lime, thinly sliced 1

Step 1: Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan and sauté the onion until soft, about 7 minutes.

Step 2: Add the tamarind paste, garlic, ginger, and jalapeños. Cook over medium-low heat for another 5 minutes.

Step 3: Add the remaining ingredients, anchovies through lime, and stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 5 hours or until thickened enough to coat the back of a spoon.

Step 4: Strain the Worcestershire sauce into glass bottles or jars and refrigerate.
